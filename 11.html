We met at a time when all I really needed was company. You lived near Chinatown, in an apartment building sandwiched between a deli and a smoke shop. Your place was on the fourth floor, I’d lose track around the third set of stairs.
The same month Carter stopped coming to work, I began calling you, almost every night, to let you know I was at your front doorstep. I’m here, I’m here.
That night, eating dinner alone, I began to feel my own chest. I looked up “side effects of coke” online—just to be fully thorough, then, “side effects of cocaine.”
We always shared your bed tentatively, as near-strangers often do.
