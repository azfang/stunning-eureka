Six years ago I coded my first ever website, titled ascii heaven.
The site was made up entirely of html, hosted on a temporary server. It translated childhood drawings from when I was six years old—maps diagramming an imaginary heaven, back when I believed in an afterlife—into ascii art.
Like the original drawings, which are long gone, the original URL for ascii heaven now only shows an error message. However, at the time of its conception ascii heaven was populated with an ascii McDonald’s, an ascii Borders bookstore, an ascii Baskin Robbins, an ascii Panda Express.
That same month, he had sent me an email (the subject line: '8/1 stuff’). No message attached, just ten or eleven links to secondhand shopping sites. He was a stylist, and sometimes he would compile shopping links when he came across certain items for me. He was always trying to get me to buy clothes he thought I’d look good in. That was his kind of love.
As a six year-old, I had simply redrawn the suburban shopping plaza near my childhood home, and called it heaven.
