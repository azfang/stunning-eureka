She’d developed a heart murmur. I was in the middle of eating breakfast with her, and forgot to be particularly comforting. I told her I liked the idea of a heart murmur. She responded that she did too.
She lived directly above me; I could hear her thumping around upstairs, from time to time. Offbeats found their way into the floors and walls of my room. Scattered footsteps, the cloying sounds of the radiator. I reveled in the constant malfunctioning; there were always many fatty rhythms to chew through.
That night, eating dinner alone, I began to feel my own chest. I looked up “side effects of coke” online—just to be fully thorough, then, “side effects of cocaine.”
One website suggested addiction, also paranoia. Addiction, paranoia, heart failure, stroke, infections, seizures, hyperthermia, arrhythmia, hallucinations, delirium.
I thought the house could collapse at any second, really.
